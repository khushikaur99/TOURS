Detailed Day Wise Itinerary

Depart from Delhi in the evening by Volvo bus (approx 8:30 PM). Overnight journey to Bhuntar.

Arrive in Bhuntar in the morning. Transfer to Kasol (approx 2 hours). Check into camps/hotel. After some rest:

  • Explore the hippie town of Kasol
  • Visit the famous Israeli cafes
  • Short walk along the Parvati River
  • Visit Manikaran Sahib Gurudwara and hot springs

Overnight stay in Kasol.

After breakfast, drive to Barshaini (1 hour), the starting point of the trek. Begin your trek to Kheerganga:

  • Moderate 12 km trek through pine forests
  • Pass through beautiful waterfalls and villages
  • Reach Kheerganga by evening
  • Relax in the natural hot water springs
  • Overnight camping under the stars

Wake up to breathtaking views. After breakfast, trek down to Barshaini:

  • Descend through different route for varied views
  • Drive back to Kasol
  • Free time to explore Kasol market
  • Overnight stay in Kasol

Morning free for last minute shopping. After lunch, drive to Bhuntar to catch Volvo bus to Delhi. Overnight journey.

Kasol Kheerganga Trek FAQs

The Kheerganga trek has different experiences in different seasons:

  • April-June: Pleasant weather (15°C to 25°C), best for first-timers
  • July-August: Monsoon season - lush greenery but slippery trails
  • September-November: Clear skies, best for photography
  • December-March: Winter trek - challenging but magical snow views

Note: The trek is closed during heavy snowfall (usually Jan-Feb).

Essential packing list for the trek:

  • Clothing: Quick-dry t-shirts, trek pants, warm layers, rain jacket
  • Footwear: Sturdy trekking shoes with good grip + flip flops
  • Accessories: Sun cap, sunglasses, headlamp, water bottle
  • Essentials: Personal medication, toiletries, power bank
  • For winter: Thermal wear, gloves, woolen socks, balaclava

Backpack weight should not exceed 8-10 kg. Porters can be arranged at extra cost.

The Kheerganga trek is considered moderate in difficulty:

  • Distance: 12 km each way (total 24 km)
  • Duration: 5-7 hours ascent, 4-5 hours descent
  • Altitude: 2,960 meters at summit
  • Terrain: Well-defined trail with some steep sections

First-time trekkers can complete it with proper pacing. We include acclimatization breaks and have emergency protocols in place.

Our general guidelines for age suitability:

  • Minimum age: 12 years (with parent/guardian)
  • Ideal range: 18-55 years for best experience
  • Seniors: Up to 65 if physically active - consult doctor first

We assess each participant's fitness level before the trek. Those with heart conditions, severe asthma, or recent surgeries are advised against attempting.

Basic facilities available at the summit:

  • Accommodation: Shared tents with sleeping bags/mats
  • Food: Basic vegetarian meals (dal, rice, roti)
  • Bathrooms: Common toilet tents (no showers)
  • Hot Springs: Separate bathing areas for men/women
  • Connectivity: No electricity, limited mobile network

We recommend carrying wet wipes, hand sanitizer, and any special snacks you might need.
