Detailed Day Wise Itinerary

Arrive in Jaipur, meet our representative and transfer to hotel. After some rest, proceed for local sightseeing covering:

  • Hawa Mahal - iconic Palace of Winds
  • City Palace - royal residence and museum
  • Jantar Mantar - astronomical observation site
  • Evening visit to local market for Rajasthani handicrafts

Overnight stay in Jaipur.

Full day exploring Jaipur's royal heritage:

  • Morning visit to Amber Fort with elephant ride
  • Jal Mahal - water palace in Man Sagar Lake
  • Nahargarh Fort - offering panoramic city views
  • Evening cultural show with dinner at Chokhi Dhani

Overnight stay in Jaipur.

After breakfast, drive to Jodhpur (340 km / 6-7 hrs). Enroute visit:

  • Ajmer Sharif Dargah - famous Sufi shrine
  • Pushkar Lake - sacred Hindu pilgrimage site

On arrival in Jodhpur, check in to hotel. Evening at leisure. Overnight stay in Jodhpur.

Rajasthan Tour FAQs

The ideal time to visit Rajasthan depends on your preferences:

  • October-March: Pleasant weather (10°C to 28°C), peak tourist season
  • April-June: Extremely hot (35°C to 48°C), but fewer crowds
  • July-September: Monsoon season, lush landscapes, some festivals
  • January: Special time for desert festivals in Jaisalmer and Bikaner

Note: Winter nights in desert areas can be quite cold (5°C or lower).

Clothing recommendations for Rajasthan:

  • Summer: Light, loose cotton clothes, wide-brimmed hat, sunglasses
  • Winter: Layered clothing, warm jacket (especially for desert nights)
  • Religious Sites: Modest clothing (shoulders and knees covered)
  • Desert: Comfortable closed shoes, scarf to protect from sand/dust

Traditional Rajasthani attire like colorful leheriya or bandhani prints blend perfectly!

Rajasthan is generally safe, but these precautions are recommended:

  • Dress modestly to avoid unwanted attention
  • Avoid isolated areas after dark
  • Use registered guides and official transportation
  • Book reputable hotels with good reviews
  • Carry a local SIM card for emergencies

Many women travelers explore Rajasthan solo, but joining group tours is also a good option.

Rajasthan offers unique culinary delights. Must-try dishes include:

  • Dal Baati Churma: Lentils with baked wheat balls and sweet crumble
  • Laal Maas: Spicy red meat curry (lamb specialty)
  • Gatte ki Sabzi: Gram flour dumplings in spicy yogurt gravy
  • Ker Sangri: Traditional desert beans and berries preparation
  • Mawa Kachori: Sweet pastry from Jodhpur

Vegetarian options are abundant, and thali meals offer complete experiences.

Rajasthan is a shopper's paradise. Top items and places:

  • Jaipur: Gemstones, blue pottery, block print textiles
  • Jodhpur: Mojris (traditional shoes), antiques
  • Jaisalmer: Leather goods, embroidered textiles
  • Udaipur: Miniature paintings, silver jewelry
  • General: Puppets, camel leather items, lac bangles

Bargaining is expected in local markets. Government emporiums offer fixed prices.
