Detailed Day Wise Itinerary

Arrive in Amritsar, meet our representative and transfer to hotel. After some rest, proceed for local sightseeing covering:

  • Golden Temple - spiritual and cultural heart of Sikhism
  • Jallianwala Bagh - historic public garden and memorial
  • Partition Museum - documenting the partition of India
  • Evening visit to local market for Punjabi cuisine

Overnight stay in Amritsar.

Full day exploring Amritsar's rich heritage:

  • Morning visit to Golden Temple for spiritual experience
  • Gobindgarh Fort - 18th century historic fort
  • Durgiana Temple - Hindu temple resembling Golden Temple
  • Wagah Border ceremony in evening (subject to schedule)

Overnight stay in Amritsar.

After breakfast, drive to Chandigarh (240 km / 5-6 hrs). Enroute visit:

  • Khalsa College - architectural marvel in Amritsar
  • Pul Kanjari - historic site on Wagah-Lahore road

On arrival in Chandigarh, check in to hotel. Evening at leisure. Overnight stay in Chandigarh.

Punjab Tour FAQs

The ideal time to visit Punjab depends on your preferences:

  • October-March: Pleasant weather (10°C to 25°C), best for sightseeing
  • April-June: Hot weather (25°C to 45°C), but good for experiencing rural Punjab
  • July-September: Monsoon season, lush green landscapes
  • November: Special time to visit during the vibrant Sikh festivals

Note: Summers can be extremely hot, while winters can be chilly, especially in Amritsar.

Clothing recommendations for Punjab:

  • Religious Sites: Modest clothing (cover head in gurdwaras, remove shoes)
  • Summer: Light cotton clothes, hat, sunglasses, sunscreen
  • Winter: Warm layers, jacket, scarf (mornings/evenings can be cold)
  • General: Comfortable walking shoes for temple visits

Traditional Punjabi attire like salwar kameez or kurta pajama is always appreciated!

Yes, visitors must follow these protocols at Harmandir Sahib (Golden Temple):

  • Cover your head (scarves provided free at entrance)
  • Remove shoes and wash feet at entrance
  • No smoking, alcohol or non-vegetarian food allowed
  • Dress modestly (shoulders and knees covered)
  • Photography allowed but be respectful

All visitors are welcome to partake in the free community kitchen (langar).

Punjab is famous for its rich culinary tradition. Must-try dishes include:

  • Amritsari Kulcha: Stuffed bread served with chole (chickpea curry)
  • Makki di Roti & Sarson da Saag: Cornbread with mustard greens
  • Tandoori Chicken: Marinated chicken cooked in clay oven
  • Lassi: Traditional yogurt-based drink (sweet or salty)
  • Punjabi Thali: Complete meal with multiple curries, breads and rice

Vegetarian options are widely available, and langar at Golden Temple is a unique experience.

Popular shopping items and places in Punjab:

  • Amritsar: Phulkari embroidery, Punjabi juttis (shoes), dry fruits
  • Chandigarh: Modern handicrafts, leather goods, designer wear
  • Traditional: Wooden toys, brassware, Punjabi musical instruments
  • Food Items: Amritsari papad, pickles, Punjabi spices

Bargaining is expected in local markets like Amritsar's Hall Bazaar.
