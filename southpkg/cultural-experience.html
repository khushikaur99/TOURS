Detailed Day Wise Itinerary

Arrive at Cochin airport and transfer to hotel. Afternoon heritage walk through Fort Kochi visiting Chinese fishing nets, St. Francis Church, and Mattancherry Palace. Evening Kathakali dance performance at cultural center. Overnight in Cochin.

Morning drive to Alleppey (2 hours). Board your private houseboat at 12 PM for backwater cruise. Visit local villages, see coir-making demonstrations, and enjoy Kerala lunch onboard. Evening sunset views from the deck. Overnight on houseboat.

Disembark houseboat after breakfast. Drive to Kumarakom (1.5 hours). Visit Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary (seasonal). Afternoon village walk to see local crafts and toddy tapping. Traditional Kerala sadya lunch on banana leaf. Overnight in Kumarakom.

Morning drive through scenic routes to Munnar (4 hours). Enroute visit Cheeyappara and Valara waterfalls. Afternoon visit to Tea Museum and tea plantation. Learn about tea processing. Overnight in Munnar.

Full day exploring Munnar: Visit Eravikulam National Park (Nilgiri Tahr habitat), Mattupetty Dam, Echo Point, and Top Station. See Neelakurinji flowers if in season (blooms every 12 years). Evening at leisure. Overnight in Munnar.

Morning drive to Thekkady (3.5 hours). Afternoon spice plantation tour - see cardamom, pepper, vanilla and coffee cultivation. Evening Kalaripayattu (martial arts) performance. Overnight in Thekkady.

Morning boat ride on Periyar Lake for wildlife spotting (elephants, tigers if lucky). Afternoon visit to tribal village and bamboo rafting (optional). Evening Ayurvedic massage experience. Overnight in Thekkady.

Morning at leisure. Transfer to Cochin airport (5 hours) or Madurai airport (4 hours) for departure with unforgettable memories of Kerala's diverse culture and landscapes.

Kerala Cultural Experience FAQs

Our cultural immersion focuses on authentic experiences:

  • Performances: Private Kathakali and Kalaripayattu shows with backstage access
  • Interactions: Meet tea pickers, spice farmers, and local artisans
  • Cuisine: Cooking classes and meals with Kerala families
  • Accommodation: Heritage stays and eco-friendly properties
  • Guides: Local experts with deep cultural knowledge

Activity levels at different locations:

  • Cochin: Light walking (1-2km/day) on flat terrain
  • Munnar: Moderate walking in tea estates (some inclines)
  • Thekkady: Optional bamboo rafting (gentle activity)
  • Alleppey: Minimal exertion on houseboat
  • Transport: Private AC vehicle between destinations

We can customize activities for mobility limitations.

Cultural norms to respect during your visit:

  • Dress Code: Modest clothing preferred (covered shoulders/knees at religious sites)
  • Temples: Remove footwear, some restrict entry for non-Hindus
  • Photography: Always ask permission before photographing people
  • Left Hand: Considered unclean - use right hand for eating/giving
  • Public Behavior: Avoid public displays of affection

Your guide will provide location-specific advice.

Immersive cultural experiences:

  • Kathakali: 2-hour show including makeup demonstration (starts 5PM)
  • Kalaripayattu: 90-minute martial arts performance with interactive session
  • Theyyam: Seasonal ritual performance in North Kerala (Dec-Feb)
  • Village Arts: Traditional music/dance by local communities

All performances have English explanations.

Dietary accommodation highlights:

  • Vegetarian: Extensive options available at all meals
  • Vegan: Traditional Kerala cuisine uses coconut oil (no ghee)
  • Jain: Special arrangements possible with advance notice
  • Allergies: Chefs accommodate common dietary restrictions

Typical vegetarian dishes: Avial, olan, thoran, sambar, idiyappam.

Festival experiences we can arrange:

  • Onam (Aug-Sep): Witness snake boat races and floral decorations
  • Vishu (Apr): Experience New Year rituals and fireworks
  • Christmas: See Kochi's Portuguese-influenced celebrations
  • Pooram (Apr-May): Thrilling temple festivals with elephants

Festival dates vary yearly - ask for specific scheduling.
